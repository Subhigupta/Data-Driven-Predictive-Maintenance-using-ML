Objective

Objective of the problem is to develop predictive maintenance models using machine learning techniques, to identify whether it is safe or not to operate a machine under a particular process condition. This enables proactive maintenance interventions, minimizing downtime, reducing maintenance costs, and maximizing operational efficiency.

Binary classification

In binary classification, the target column contains values 0 and 1, indicating whether the machine has failed for a given datapoint.

  • 0 indicates no failure.
  • 1 indicates a failure has occurred.

Multinomial classification

In multinomial clasification, the target column contains following six failure modes:

  • Power Failure: Issues related to power supply interruptions or electrical faults.
  • Tool Wear Failure: Wear and degradation of machine tools leading to malfunction.
  • Overstrain Failure: Excessive mechanical stress or load causing system breakdown.
  • Random Failure: Failures occurring unpredictably without a clear cause.
  • Heat Dissipation Failure: Inability to manage heat, leading to overheating and potential damage.
  • No Failure: Indicating that equipment is in healthy state.
